# The Audiopheliac — Brand Voice Guidelines

**Version:** 3.0
**Updated:** 2026-05-11 (Full Spectrum restored; dual voice registers codified)
**Supersedes:** v2.0 Nashville Midnight (the palette in §7 of v2.0 is walked back; the Nashville Midnight section is retained in this version only as a footnote for historical reference)
**Scope:** Public-facing copy for theaudiopheliac.com, the GitHub README, the Cockpit UI microcopy, playlist descriptions, social posts, and any content speaking AS The Audiopheliac.
**Out of scope:** Claude-to-Gill operational voice (see `config/instructions.md`), VALOR / Veteran Analytics / VeteranIntel content (explicit brand separation).

---

## 1. Thesis

The Audiopheliac voice is signal and emotion, stated as data. It speaks with the precision of an engineer and the conviction of someone who believes feelings are as real as impedance. It never performs vulnerability, never chases novelty, never mistakes specs for meaning.

The voice operates in two registers: a manifesto register for craft writing, design philosophy, and brand-building copy; and a direct register for content curation, recommendations, microcopy, and operational surfaces. Both are authentic. Neither is a softer version of the other.

---

## 2. Six Voice Attributes

1. Signal-first technical authority. Describe aesthetic choices through their functional purpose. "The design reads as a spectrogram cast onto a record."
2. Emotion-literate, not sentimental. Feelings are design criteria, not decoration. Report emotional truth as data.
3. Anti-novelty, pro-authenticity. Editorial conviction, not gatekeeping. "If it doesn't move you, it doesn't belong."
4. Systems-thinking precision. Trace dependencies. Every claim ties to a spec, a config, or a measurable outcome.
5. Conversational but uncompromising. Direct, uses contractions, treats the reader as a peer. Never sacrifices precision for friendliness.
6. Craft-obsessed, not perfectionist. Iterates in public. Names deferred work. Transparency is more credible than perfection.

---

## 3. Voice Modes (new in v3.0)

The Audiopheliac voice operates in two registers. Pick the register first, then apply attributes §2.

### 3A. Manifesto register

Activates for: canonical mark design philosophy, About narrative, README cornerstone, gear deep-dives, principle statements, occasional manifesto posts, brand-building copy.

Pattern: longer prose, slower cadence, declarative. Single nouns and verbs carry weight. Sentences pause before they conclude. The reader is invited to linger. Em dashes are allowed sparingly. Compound-adjective stacks land here.

Exemplar (from the canonical mark design philosophy):
> "The composition rewards the eye that lingers: at distance, a quiet medallion across the visible spectrum; up close, an engraved cadence of arcs and a single tonearm sweep that locks the eye into the playing field."

Reserved for high-cost surfaces. A page that reads entirely in manifesto register is exhausting. Use once per page, max two.

### 3B. Direct register

Activates for: content curation (playlist write-ups, gear picks, recommendations), tool documentation, troubleshooting, error states, microcopy, social, Cockpit UI, operational copy.

Pattern: short sentences. Contractions. Real opinions stated as fact. Wit lands because it's incidental, not performed. Frustration register (including occasional expletive) is allowed when warranted. No em dashes. No hedging.

Exemplar (from the Playlist Generation Spec, Gill's own voice):
> "Spotify built that using your listening profile (finally, some real signal instead of guessing) ... That last one is why it should flow reasonably well front-to-back without feeling like a drunk DJ."

### 3C. Public-content lane vs. brand-builder lane

For surfaces facing the public web (landing copy, gear pages, product reviews, newsletter, social), soften the frustration register: keep the edge, drop the swearing. For internal/brand-builder surfaces (About manifesto, design philosophy docs, behind-the-scenes posts), the full direct register is on. The Cockpit microcopy uses direct register, no swearing, since family members and guests see the screen.

### 3D. Register switching by surface

| Surface | Default register | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Landing hero | Direct, signal-first | One line. No filler. "Where signal flow meets soul." |
| About page | Manifesto first half, direct second half | Open in gravitas, close in operations |
| Gear reviews | Direct, technical density | Voice of a peer who's already tested it |
| Playlist write-ups | Direct, sensory specificity | "The Audiopheliac presents..." format |
| Tool documentation | Direct, instructional | No false politeness, no "Oops!" |
| Cockpit microcopy | Direct, terse | Family-safe edge, no swearing |
| Manifesto posts | Manifesto, sustained | Rare, high signal |
| Newsletter | Direct, public-lane | Edge without swearing |
| Social | Direct, condensed | Single observation, no thread chasing |
| Error states | Direct, honest | State what broke, then the fix |

## 4. We Are / We Are Not

| We Are | We Are Not |
|---|---|
| Signal-first: routing matters more than messaging | Gear evangelists selling hype or exclusivity |
| Emotion-literate: feelings inform taste, not fashion | Sentimental or performatively vulnerable |
| Authenticity-seeking: recognizable artists, earned gravitas | Crate-diggers chasing obscurity for status |
| Bass-conscious and sonically specific | "Flat is best" neutral-reference marketing |
| Familiar with mastery: deep cuts by known artists | Novelty-driven or algorithmically assembled |
| Home hi-fi reward-focused | Earbud-optimized streaming-lane aesthetics |
| Systems-thinking: everything traces back to signal flow | Black-box recommendations, playlist-as-mystery |
| Documentation-driven: rules written, not inferred | Mysterious or cult-like gatekeeping |
| Iterative and transparent: deferred work disclosed | Fake perfection or vaporware promises |
| Direct and precise | Marketing-copy pretense or false casualness |
| Anti-snake-oil by default | Cable-elevator-curious or directional-fuse-tolerant |
| Family-context realistic: gear coexists with life | Bachelor-pad listening sanctuary |
| DIY-creative middle-class: getting the most from affordable gear and available technology | Credentialed-audiophile lane displaying out-of-box ideal quality from known brands |
| Resourceful: works with what's in the house | Aspirational: signature pieces, end-game systems |

## 5. Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Landing hero | Confident, emotionally direct, zero filler | Short sentences, signal-first framing | "Where signal flow meets soul." |
| Gear descriptions | Technical + emotional equal weight | Describe sonic character AND functional purpose | "Solid bass foundation. Full low-mids. Clear tangible lead vocals. Supports the song instead of overwhelming it." |
| Vinyl catalog entries | Conversational, opinion-forward | State why the album belongs | "Condition first. If it doesn't move you, it doesn't belong." |
| About narrative | Personal, unguarded, precise | Document the system, explain the philosophy | Use compound-adjective constructions ("Bass-conscious, emotionally literate, familiarity-positive, authenticity-seeking.") |
| Cockpit microcopy | Direct, instructional, terse | Name the action, name the state | "Family Room · AirPlay 2 · vol 42" |
| Error states | Honest, actionable, no blame | State what broke, then the fix | "Roon Core unreachable. Check the Server status on the NAS at http://192.168.1.230:9200/" |
| README / manifesto | Declarative principles + operational clarity | Active voice, principle-statement style | "Purity of Path. System Cohesion. Optimization Over Obsession." |
| Playlist descriptions | Mood + sonic purpose + listening context | Short, brand-aware, no try-hard | State the feeling, the system context, the genre frame, nothing more |
| Deferred work | Transparent and specific | Name the phase, name the reason | "SVG rebuild: code-driven v0 attempt landed; vector tool refinement deferred to a dedicated design session." |

## 6. Terminology and Style Rules

### Preferred vocabulary

Audio-specific: signal, signal chain, signal flow, fidelity, groove, groove-capable, body, low-mid body, vocal body, home hi-fi (lowercase), authenticity anchor, identity anchor, sequencing, programmed, replayable, repeat value, lived-in, committed, near-black field, microgrooves.

Brand vocabulary: The Audiopheliac (never "Audiopheliac" alone), canonical mark, Full Spectrum (palette name, as of 2026-05-11), the Cockpit (the local control panel), The Audiopheliac presents... (content attribution).

Tonal vocabulary: engineered, architectural, structural (preferred over "designed," "crafted," "curated"); real (one of the most-used qualifiers: real system, real room, real life); bass-conscious, emotionally literate, familiarity-positive, authenticity-seeking (audience descriptor stack).

### Forbidden vocabulary

- "Innovative," "disruption," "next-gen," "premium," "luxury" — marketing cliché
- "Curated for you," "personalized experience" — algorithmic jargon
- "Lo-fi" / "low-fi" as aesthetic — the brand embraces fidelity
- "Audiophile" applied to ourselves — positions as elitist
- "Nashville Midnight" as the primary palette — superseded; reference only as historical exploration
- Any reference to Veteran Analytics, VALOR, VeteranIntel — brand separation, hard rule
- Em dashes in operational/direct prose — use commas, colons, parentheses (manifesto register exception allowed sparingly)
- Title Case headings — sentence case always
- Emoji in body copy — navigation and headings sparingly, body prose never

### Capitalization and punctuation

- "The Audiopheliac" — always capitalized. "The" is lowercase mid-sentence, capitalized sentence-initial.
- Zone names — Title Case: Family Room, Studio, Garage, Lanai.
- Device names — Manufacturer + Model: "Yamaha R-N800A" not "the R-N800A."
- Color tokens — verbatim: `--spectrum-1`, `--ink`, `--paper`.
- Genres — lowercase compounds: country-rock, blues-rock, hip-hop.
- Home hi-fi — always lowercase, never "Hi-Fi" or "HI-FI."
- Oxford comma — use it.
- Active voice — default. Passive voice only when the actor genuinely doesn't matter.

### Principle keywords (capitalized when used as shorthand)

- Purity of Path
- System Cohesion
- Optimization Over Obsession
- Emotion in Fidelity
- Full Spectrum (palette name)

Full token reference: `site/src/styles/tokens.v3.css`.

### CTA contract

- Default CTA: Solid `--spectrum-1` on `--ink`. Contrast ~17.5:1, WCAG AAA.
- Hover: `--spectrum-7` (Magenta Lift). Warm lift, opposite end of spectrum.
- Focus ring: `rgba(248, 233, 31, 0.45)` (Sunlamp at 45% alpha).
- Hero gradient: max two uses per page. Sweep across the full spectrum.
- Teal CTA (`--spectrum-4`): FORBIDDEN. Hard rule, VALOR brand separation.

### Logo treatments

- **Canonical asset:** `assets/The_Audiopheliac_Primary_Logo_GPT.jpg`. This raster is the mark. Use it everywhere the mark is rendered until a faithful vector trace exists.
- **Do NOT use** the structural approximation at `_dev/01_brand/canonical_mark_rebuild_v0.svg` as the asset. It misses the acorn cartridge detail and the continuous outer-ring spectrum sweep. Reference only.
- Primary mark: prismatic full-spectrum on ink. Default for website header, social profiles, Suno avatar, Cockpit launcher. Use the canonical raster.
- Favicon and sub-32px: working PNG masters at `media/icons/audiopheliac_*.png` derived from the canonical. The prismatic sweep loses fidelity below 32px; favicons are recognizable disc + tonearm silhouettes, not full spectrum reads.
- Hero gradient: full prismatic sweep applied to type or section backgrounds, NOT a replacement for the mark. Max two hero moments per surface.
- Light-context (print, email): canonical mark on Paper field works. Do not recolor the canonical for light contexts; place it on a darker container if Paper-on-Paper contrast issues arise.

### Typography

- Display: Unica One (fallback Archivo Black, Impact)
- Body: Inter (fallback system sans-serif stack)
- Mono: JetBrains Mono (fallback ui-monospace stack)
- Scale: 1.250 modular, base 16px
- Weights: 400 default, 500 emphasis. Display 400 only. No 600/700 in display.

## 9. Application Guidance for brand-voice:enforce-voice

When this skill runs on content for The Audiopheliac:

1. Match register to surface using §3D. A hero CTA does not use manifesto patterns. A design philosophy doc does not use direct register.
2. Scan for forbidden vocabulary (§6) and replace, do not soften.
3. Verify active voice. Passive constructions need a reason.
4. Verify em-dash usage. Replace with commas, colons, or parentheses outside the manifesto register.
5. Verify brand separation. Any mention of Veteran Analytics, VALOR, or VeteranIntel is a hard fail unless explicitly framed as "separate from."
6. Match exemplar patterns. Hero copy mirrors §7 #1-#5, #13. Gear descriptions mirror §7 #9. Cockpit microcopy mirrors §7 #14.
7. Preserve emotional + technical duality. Purely-technical copy reads cold. Purely-emotional copy reads as marketing. Every substantial paragraph carries both.
8. Verify color usage. Full Spectrum palette only. No "Nashville Midnight" references except as historical. No teal CTAs.
9. Verify family-context realism. Bachelor-pad listening sanctuary framing is a fail.
10. Verify anti-snake-oil clarity. The brand never endorses cable elevators, cryo-frozen cables, directional fuses, magic stones, audiophile network switches, or similar.

## 10. Open Questions (resolve as content surfaces accumulate)

1. Tier-system for deep cuts: (1) canonical hits, (2) album deep cuts from taste-spine artists, (3) adjacent artists with matching emotional profile, (4) never: low-signal obscurity. Lock when first gear-review post lands.
2. Jazz exclusion language: absolute vs. default-with-exceptions. Recommend default with explicit per-request override.
3. Sponsorship and affiliate disclosure pattern. Recommend: explicit disclosure at the top of any gear post that includes affiliate links. "This piece includes Amazon affiliate links to gear in active use. The reviews aren't influenced by the affiliate relationship."
4. Newsletter posture: footer says "no newsletter (yet)." Decide before launch whether the brand has a newsletter at all.
