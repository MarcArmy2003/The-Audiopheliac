## Executive Summary
Gill's listening taste is centered on emotion-first, groove-capable, mainstream-adjacent music with real low-end payoff on home hi-fi playback. He prefers music that is recognizable, substantial, and replayable rather than obscure, self-consciously arty, or novelty-driven. His strongest gravity wells are modern country, classic and hard rock, blues and blues-rock, and selective hip-hop and crossover pop. He tends to favor songs that sound lived-in, committed, and physically rewarding on a full-range system.

The most reliable structural lane is:
- Country and country-adjacent
- Classic rock and hard rock
- Blues, blues-rock, and soul-blues
- Selective hip-hop and crossover pop

### 2. Genre weighting guidance
Approximate relative importance for playlist design:
- Country / country-adjacent: Very high
- Classic rock / hard rock: Very high
- Blues / blues-rock / roots: High

- Pop / crossover pop: Moderate, when emotionally direct or sonically substantial

He does not prefer:

- crate-digging obscurity in streaming mode

### 4. Emotional profile

- regret
- longing
- swagger

Preferred songs tend to commit emotionally rather than perform detachment, irony, or abstraction.

## Sonic Priorities

### 1. Playback priorities
Gill's playback profile strongly favors:
- solid bass foundation
- full low mids
- clear, tangible lead vocals
- muscular drum presence
- guitars with texture and body
- arrangements that remain full and satisfying at moderate listening levels

### 2. Bass preference subtype
The desired bass profile is structural and grounding rather than club-oriented.
Target qualities:
- foundation
- propulsion
- warmth
- authority
- body that supports the song instead of overwhelming it
Avoid:
- bass gimmicks
- exaggerated sub-bass without musical payoff
- brittle "loud equals exciting" mastering choices

### 3. Hi-fi reward factors
Tracks that tend to fit best:
- strong center vocal image
- kick and bass that lock together
- low-mid density without mud
- room-filling texture
- enough dynamic contour to reward real speakers
- songs that sound more convincing when the system opens up

## What a Good Audiopheliac Playlist Should Do
A high-fit playlist for Gill should:
- start with rooted warmth or groove rather than immediate maximalism
- use country, blues-rock, and classic rock as the structural backbone
- add hip-hop and pop strategically for rhythmic or tonal contrast
- keep artist repetition controlled
- prefer tracks with real vocal body and low-end authority
- feel coherent from start to finish rather than recommendation-clustered
- reward home hi-fi listening, especially bass integration and room presence
- end with emotional weight or satisfying glide-out rather than random drop-off

## What a Weak Playlist Usually Does
A weak-fit playlist for Gill will:
- over-index on obscure discoveries
- drift into generic indie or art-first curation
- stack too much high-energy metal or radio hard rock in one run
- confuse loudness with bass quality
- ignore sequencing and tonal fatigue
- include too many songs that are "fine" but not identity-level
- feel generated instead of programmed

## Canonical Preference Rules for Future Use

### Prefer
- recognizable artists
- hits and popular deep cuts
- emotionally direct songs
- songs with bass weight and low-mid body
- country, classic rock, hard rock, blues-rock, roots, selective hip-hop, selective pop

### Avoid by default
- classical
- jazz in general listening playlists unless explicitly requested

- covers and AI renditions: acceptable if popular, recognized, and actually fit the brief
